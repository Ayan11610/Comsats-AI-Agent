# COMSATS University Islamabad — Abbottabad Campus

## Overview of COMSATS
The Third World Academy of Sciences (TWAS) initiated the proposal for the formation of the Commission on Science and Technology for Sustainable Development in the South (COMSATS). At that time, TWAS was headed by Nobel Laureate Prof. Dr. Abd-ul-Salam from Pakistan. On his suggestion, the Foundation Conference was held in October 1994 in Islamabad. Representatives from 36 countries and various international organizations attended. The Conference unanimously adopted the resolution for the establishment of COMSATS.

COMSATS is an international organization headquartered in Islamabad, with the main objective of promoting science and technology in the development process of developing countries. Since its inception, COMSATS has supported programs for science and technology development in member countries.

---

## Abbottabad Campus Overview
- **Location:** Abbottabad, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan  
- **Campus Area:** 308 Kanals (current campus), 486 Kanals allocated at Dhamtour for permanent campus  
- **Established:** Became functional in July 2001; first academic session in September 2001  
- **Student Strength:** 5,672 (initially 121 students)  
- **Departments:** 14 (initially 3)  
- **Programs:** 15 undergraduate, 24 graduate programs  
- **Faculty Strength:** 386, including 212+ PhDs  
- **Laboratories:** 90 modern laboratories  

The campus has grown from a modest beginning with old World War II barracks to a leading regional institute, offering quality education, state-of-the-art facilities, and contributing to research in Pakistan. The motto of the campus is **“We Aim to Inspire.”**

---

The Dhamtour Campus aims to provide modern, well-equipped facilities for students and staff while contributing to local and national development.
